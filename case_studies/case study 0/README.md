# Case study 0: A child-centred AI ecosystem to transform early year education

## The original design goal

Create a tangible childhood that begins with children feeling seen, supported and safe to explore, grow and belong. 

Build a child-centred AI ecosystem designed to transform early education into a safer, fairer, and smarter public good.

#### Step 1: Assessment — Design Context and Agency Intent

The design team chose to focus on the “wildcard” function of the system for 4–5-year-old users, who expressed anxiety when their teachers discussed planning for summer holidays, which conflict with their parents’ commitments.

While children's agency has been a priority for the design team, using CHAI's **agency conceptulisation framework**, the team externalised nuanced considerations of agency, including
- co-agency: to support teachers and children's co-planning of holidays
- collective agency: to use friends' holiday planning to mitigate anxieties
- proxy agency: to use AI to gain additional support for holiday planning

The identification of co-agency and collective agency has been particulary prominent in the process.

#### Step 2: Mapping — Agency Annotation

While new types of agency have been identified in the above process, the design team believed that individual and proxy agencies are still the most important types of agency for them to support in the system.

#### Step 3: Application — Critical Redesign 

The application process has led to an exciting, explorative design brainstorming process. The team considered several new functions, including
- Adding a 'memo board' to facilitate the co-engagement between teachers and children
- Enhancing AI agents' ability to recognise children's speech and expressions to ensure children's individual agency
- Adding a wildcard collection and sharing function to enhance collective agency and mitigate children's individual anxiety
- Developing a child-centred AI evaluation framework, shifting from system-centric/accuracy focused evaluation to child-in-the-loop assessment, to ensure children's agency and safety.

#### Step 4: Reflection — Reasoning Review

The workshop has led the team to have a deeper thinking about ***the balance of agency types*** in their system. However, they also raised the questions about how to map these possibilities with an organisational, technical, or pedagogical justification, and how they may fit into what children would like to have from the system.

### After the Workshop

At the end of the workshop, the design team expressed their follow-up:
- Choose one or two gaps or tensions to focus on and assess their impact and feasibility.
- Adjust interaction design, data governance, or data flow to rebalance agency.
- Identify success metrics for each design iteration.

This case study shows that CHAI plays an effective role of enabling a design team to externalise the reasoning of designing for agency more systmatically. However, we must highlight that CHAI makes the start of the journey and can be iteratively applied to one's design context to achieve the real impact.
