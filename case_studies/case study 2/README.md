# Case study 2: An AI-based tool to support children's social emotional learning

## The original design goal

The goal is to support children's social emotional learning by taking different perspectives of a real-life social situation scenario.


#### Step 1: Assessment — Design Context and Agency Intent

The team chose to focus on the scenario where two chidlren have fought for toys many times, and the parents want the children to use the AI tool with them to learn how to understand each other's perspectives.


#### Step 2: Mapping — Agency Annotation

The team believe that co-agency is the most important type of agency in their system.

#### Step 3: Application — Critical Redesign 

As a result of this mapping exercise, the team added two critical functions to their existing system design:
- One allows parents and children to choose different perspectives and options generated by AI, with an option to propose their own options.
- The other one allowed parents and chidren to change the character and roles in the storyline to give children a voice

#### Step 4: Reflection — Reasoning Review

The team reflected that the workshop made them further reflect whether they were pre-defining "the agency of the parent or child when logging distress data". Children might feel "frightened" by being recorded without consent during their interactions with their system, highlighting a tension between the "parent's right to record" and the "child's right to privacy", and thus revised their original design.
