# Case study 0: An AI-based application to support children with special education needs

## The original design goal

The goal is to create an AI-based system to provide insights about the behaviour of (usually neurodivergent) children and how it relates to their environment.

The application could be use at school classrooms, at home, or in clinical settings, so that all meltdown moments are recorded.

#### Step 1: Assessment — Design Context and Agency Intent

#### Step 2: Mapping — Agency Annotation

During the mapping process, the team believe that their current system design presumes that co-agency, proxy agency, and collective agency are higher priority than children's individual agency

This process made them realise that while they are designing a system to support children's behavioural management, children's indvidual agency has been less priorities in their current design thinking.

#### Step 3: Application — Critical Redesign 

As a result of this mapping exercise, the team added a feedback loop, to provide system analysis back to the children, in an age-appropriate language. In this way, children are also provided with an overview of their behavioural analysis and be better engaged in the support process.

#### Step 4: Reflection — Reasoning Review

The design team reflected that they originally chose a parent-only input model because children aged 3–6 were seen as unable to ``articulate the problem clearly''.

However, the workshop made them think through from both sides. While this has made the design process more complicated, they noted that this complexity provided a ``more comprehensive picture of the user journey''.
