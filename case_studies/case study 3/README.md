# Case study 3: An AI-based too to support children's social emotional learning

## The original design goal

The goal is to help parents/clinicians/children understand social emotional learning (SEL) and create an environment for children in social emotional situation.


#### Step 1: Assessment — Design Context and Agency Intent

The team chose to focus on the scenario where the parent sees a child is flighting with peers and having conflicts and want to educate them about SEL in the scenario just happened so that children can control their emotion and behaviours better through the application.

To achieve this, the system can generate a personalsied story to parents and children to share and read together. Parents are also provided with theories and practical tips to support their children's SEL.

#### Step 2: Mapping — Agency Annotation

The team produced a detailed mapping of agency at different stages of the system flow, with individual and co-agency generally high.
#### Step 3: Application — Critical Redesign 

As a result of this mapping exercise, the team added two critical functions to their existing system design:
- One allows children to provide more inputs to influence the personalised stories to be generated by the system, to give the children a voice
- The other one is to create a database/record of children's issues, to be more personalised to children's individual needs


#### Step 4: Reflection — Reasoning Review

During the reflection, the team discussed that while using ``structured questions'' in the system enable participation from children with limited verbal skills, they also brought a possibility of overlooking agency of choice. 


They also reflected that using the framework to address some of the design tensions made the process ``not complicated, but complex,'' leading to a more ``refined'' and ``comprehensive'' system that respects both stakeholders.
